## The Art of Luxury Gifting

Luxury gifts are about more than price—they're about intention:

- **Choose heritage brands**: Le Creuset, Montblanc, Rimowa, and Bang & Olufsen have decades of craftsmanship behind every product
- **Prioritize quality over flash**: The best luxury gifts are those that justify their price through materials, engineering, and longevity
- **Consider their daily life**: A $500 Dyson they use every morning delivers more joy than a $1,000 decorative piece they rarely notice
- **Presentation matters**: Luxury gifts deserve luxury presentation—proper wrapping, a handwritten card, and thoughtful timing

## Pro Tips for Luxury Gifting

1. **Don't apologize for the price**: If you've decided to splurge, own it—luxury gifts are meant to feel extravagant
2. **Research before buying**: At these price points, getting the wrong color, size, or model is expensive. Ask casually or check their existing collection
3. **Buy from authorized retailers**: Luxury goods from unauthorized sellers may be counterfeit. Stick to official stores and authorized dealers
4. **Consider the recipient's style**: A minimalist won't appreciate flashy branding. Choose products whose luxury is in the quality, not just the logo
5. **Keep the receipt discreetly**: Even with luxury gifts, exchanges happen. Keep the receipt without making it obvious

The best luxury gifts aren't just expensive—they're exceptional. They're the products people would never buy for themselves but treasure for years once they have them.
