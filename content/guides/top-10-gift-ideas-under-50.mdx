## How to Make $50 Gifts Feel Thoughtful

The $50 range gives you room to be creative with presentation:

- **Bundle complementary items**: A Chemex + a bag of local roasted beans makes a complete coffee experience under $60
- **Add a personal note about why**: "I know you lose your keys every morning" with an AirTag shows you pay attention
- **Choose their color**: When a product comes in multiple colors, picking their favorite shows extra thought
- **Skip the gift receipt**: Confidence in your choice communicates that you know them well

## Pro Tips for Mid-Range Gifting

1. **Buy quality over quantity**: One great $45 item beats three mediocre $15 items every time
2. **Think about daily use**: The best gifts in this range are things people use every single day—water bottles, speakers, kitchen tools
3. **Avoid trendy unless they're into it**: A Lodge skillet will be relevant in 50 years; a trendy gadget might not be relevant in 5 months
4. **Check for sales**: Many items on this list regularly go on sale, which means you can pair them with a smaller gift and stay under $50 total
5. **Presentation matters**: A $45 gift wrapped beautifully feels more premium than a $60 gift in a plastic bag

Great gifts aren't about the price tag—they're about choosing something that fits the person. At $50, you have enough budget to find something genuinely perfect.
